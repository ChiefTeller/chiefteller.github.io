Dual Market State

The Dual Market State is a state model by Nadav Teller that proposes a new synthesis: a free market alongside a stable civic track, supported by national production, a dual public bank, AI infrastructure, affordable housing and long-term social balance.

Core idea

Freedom without abandonment. Stability without coercion.

The model does not attempt to abolish capitalism or return to a centralized socialist state. It attempts to build a dual structure in which economic freedom and civic security can exist together.

Definition

What is the Dual Market State?

The Dual Market State is a political-economic model designed to address one of the deepest structural problems of modern societies: the constant historical movement between extreme capitalism and excessive state centralization. Human societies have repeatedly moved between two unstable poles. On one side, free markets create innovation, entrepreneurship, competition and economic growth, but when left without sufficient social balance they may also produce extreme inequality, housing crises, social fragmentation and the erosion of the middle class. On the other side, centralized state systems may attempt to correct social instability, but often create bureaucracy, stagnation, political dependency and a reduction of personal freedom.

The Dual Market State proposes a different path. Instead of choosing one side of the historical pendulum, it creates two parallel tracks within the same state. The first track is the free market: private ownership, entrepreneurship, competition, innovation and personal economic freedom. The second track is the civic track: a stable public system that provides access to housing, work, education, training, infrastructure, health, energy and basic economic security.

The model does not seek to eliminate the private market. On the contrary, it recognizes that the free market is one of the most powerful engines of human creativity and technological development. However, it also argues that a society cannot depend entirely on market forces for the basic stability of its citizens. A person should be free to compete, build, earn and create wealth — but should not be forced to live under permanent economic fear merely in order to survive.

The central idea is simple: the state must create a stable floor, not an imposed ceiling. The civic track gives citizens a base of stability, while the free market remains open for ambition, innovation and private success. The state does not prevent people from rising higher. It simply ensures that falling does not mean destruction.

Historical problem

The historical pendulum between capitalism and centralization

Political history can be understood as a pendulum. When free markets become too aggressive, wealth tends to concentrate, housing becomes inaccessible, labor loses bargaining power and citizens begin to feel abandoned. In response, societies often demand stronger state intervention, regulation, welfare and public ownership. But when the state becomes too heavy, it may suppress initiative, create inefficiency, expand bureaucracy and weaken the very creative forces that produce growth.

This movement is not merely ideological. It is structural. Capitalism and socialism are not simply moral opposites; they are historical reactions to one another. Each system emerges as a correction to the failures of the other, and each system, when pushed too far, produces new failures of its own.

The Dual Market State attempts to break this cycle by building balance into the architecture of the state itself. Instead of waiting for one system to collapse and then replacing it with its opposite, the dual model allows both principles to coexist: freedom and stability, competition and security, private initiative and civic infrastructure.

The civic track

A stable civic path inside the state

The civic track is the second pillar of the Dual Market State. It is a structured public pathway that allows citizens to live, work, learn and develop within a stable national system without being fully dependent on the volatility of the private market.

The civic track may include affordable housing, public employment, vocational training, civic education, health services, energy access, public transportation, digital infrastructure and long-term personal development. It is not designed as a passive welfare system. It is designed as a productive and active system in which citizens participate in the national economy while receiving a stable foundation.

A person may choose to remain fully in the private market. Another may choose to join the civic track temporarily during a period of crisis, retraining or transition. Another may choose the civic track as a long-term lifestyle. The model is based on choice, not coercion.

This is one of the major differences between the Dual Market State and older centralized models. The civic track does not abolish the market. It creates an alternative path inside the state, so that citizens are not forced into a single economic game whose rules are controlled entirely by private capital.

National production

The National Productive Corporation

A central component of the Dual Market State is the National Productive Corporation. This is not a traditional government office and not a fully private corporation. It is a national productive structure that operates in strategic industries and generates long-term value for the civic system.

Modern states often depend mainly on taxes, debt and external markets. The Dual Market State argues that this is structurally weak. A state that only taxes wealth but does not produce wealth becomes increasingly dependent on the private sector and on financial markets. The national corporation changes this relationship. It allows the state to become a productive actor, not merely a regulator.

The corporation may operate in strategic sectors such as energy, advanced industry, artificial intelligence, robotics, construction, modular housing, agriculture, water, public transportation, data infrastructure, public cloud systems, educational technology, medical technology and national logistics.

The purpose is not to eliminate private companies. Private companies continue to compete, innovate and grow. The national corporation exists to ensure that essential productive capacity remains connected to the public good and does not fully depend on external corporate interests.

The corporation generates money by producing and selling energy, technology, infrastructure services, construction capacity, AI services, agricultural products, exports, public logistics and long-term industrial output. Instead of treating the state as an entity that only spends money, the model treats the state as an entity capable of producing value and reinvesting that value back into citizens.

This creates what the model calls a non-liquid capital loop. In a normal economy, money often leaks out of the national system through speculation, rent extraction, debt interest, external corporations and capital flight. In the dual system, part of the capital remains inside a closed productive loop: citizens work, consume, save and borrow within the civic system; the bank finances infrastructure and housing; the national corporation produces goods and services; profits return to the system; the system reinvests in housing, education, technology and productive capacity.

The goal of the non-liquid capital loop is not to imprison citizens or prevent private wealth. It is to prevent the total leakage of national productive energy. The state creates a long-term internal engine that continuously strengthens the civic track.

Financial architecture

The Dual Public Bank

The Dual Public Bank is the financial heart of the model. In the modern world, whoever controls credit controls the future. Housing, infrastructure, industry, education, technology and cities are all built through credit. If credit is controlled only by private profit motives, then the long-term direction of society is shaped primarily by financial return rather than civic stability.

The Dual Public Bank does not replace private banks. Private banks may continue to exist, compete and serve the private market. The dual bank creates a parallel public financial mechanism whose purpose is to finance long-term national development: housing, energy, industry, AI infrastructure, civic employment, education, agriculture and public transportation.

Its role is to keep capital moving inside the national productive loop. In a regular banking system, a citizen pays interest to a bank, and the profit may flow to shareholders, investment funds or external financial actors. In the dual model, credit issued through the public bank finances productive assets that belong to the civic system. The returns do not disappear into purely external financial extraction; they strengthen the national corporation, the civic track and the next generation of infrastructure.

The bank is therefore part of the non-liquid capital loop. It converts savings, public capital and productive revenue into long-term investment. Instead of treating money as a purely liquid speculative asset, the model ties part of national capital to real productive systems: housing, energy, education, food, AI, transportation and infrastructure.

This is especially important in the age of artificial intelligence. AI may concentrate wealth faster than any previous technology. If the ownership of AI infrastructure remains entirely private and global, states may lose the ability to provide stability to their citizens. A dual public bank can finance national AI infrastructure and ensure that at least part of the productivity gains produced by automation return to society as a whole.

Housing

Affordable housing as civic infrastructure

Housing is one of the central crises of the modern world. In many countries, even working citizens cannot afford stable housing. The Dual Market State argues that housing should not be treated only as a financial asset or investment instrument. It is a basic civic infrastructure.

The model proposes that the civic system may build, finance and manage affordable housing through the national corporation and the dual bank. By using modular construction, industrialized building methods, AI-based planning, public land management and long-term financing, housing costs can be reduced structurally rather than merely subsidized.

The goal is not to destroy the private housing market. People may still buy, sell, invest and build privately. But the existence of a stable civic housing track prevents the entire population from being held hostage by speculation, debt and scarcity.

AI and governance

Artificial intelligence as civic infrastructure

The Dual Market State views artificial intelligence as one of the most important forces shaping the future of governance. AI can optimize energy systems, transportation, education, agriculture, logistics, health, banking and public administration. But if AI is controlled only by private corporations, it may intensify inequality and concentrate power.

In the dual model, AI becomes part of civic infrastructure. It helps the state become a learning system: measuring outcomes, detecting inefficiency, reducing corruption, planning cities, improving education, matching citizens to training, forecasting infrastructure needs and supporting transparent governance.

However, the model also requires restraint. AI should not become a tool of total surveillance or social control. Its purpose is to increase systemic intelligence while preserving human freedom, privacy and civic dignity.

Motivation

Why would citizens join the civic track?

Citizens may join the civic track because it offers real advantages: stable housing, predictable credit, work opportunities, education, health access, civic belonging, retraining, protection from collapse and a path of personal development. The model does not assume that all citizens want the same life. Some will prefer maximum market freedom. Others will prefer stability, community and lower risk.

The dual structure allows both types of citizens to live within the same state. It does not force everyone into one ideology. It allows different economic personalities to coexist.

The deeper promise of the model is that citizens are no longer forced to choose between total market exposure and total state dependency. They can move between tracks, recover from failure, retrain, start again and participate in a system designed not only for competition, but also for continuity.

Open questions

Criticism and challenges

The Dual Market State raises serious questions. How can such a system prevent bureaucracy? How can it avoid corruption? How can the national corporation remain efficient? How can the dual bank avoid political misuse? How can the civic track remain empowering rather than passive? How can the state avoid creating two separate classes of citizens?

These are not minor questions. They are central to the model. The theory assumes that any large institution can decay if it lacks transparency, rotation, measurement, competition, civic oversight and strong internal correction mechanisms.

Therefore, the Dual Market State depends on governance design. It requires transparent data, AI-assisted auditing, public reporting, institutional rotation, limited tenure in key management roles, anti-corruption structures and continuous evaluation of outcomes.

The model should not be understood as a finished utopia. It is a proposed framework for solving structural problems that existing systems struggle to solve.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Dual Market State socialism?

No. The model preserves the free market, private property, entrepreneurship and competition. It adds a parallel civic track and national productive infrastructure.

Is it capitalism?

Not in the purely classical sense. It accepts market freedom, but argues that a modern state must also maintain civic stability and national productive capacity.

Does the model force people into the civic track?

No. The civic track is designed as an option. Citizens may choose the private market, the civic track, or move between them.

What is the non-liquid capital loop?

It is a national economic loop in which part of the capital remains tied to productive civic assets such as housing, energy, industry, AI, education and infrastructure instead of leaking outward through speculation or external extraction.

What makes the model relevant to AI?

AI may concentrate productivity and wealth. The Dual Market State attempts to ensure that part of AI-driven productivity becomes civic infrastructure rather than only private corporate power.
